About Tyler

I like to think of myself as calm and laid-back; though I can get a little spontaneous when the moment calls for it. I’ve lived in Tennessee my whole life; born, raised, and still here; and most of my days are spent grinding through long, exhausting work hours. But when the day ends, I love retreating to my little corner of the world; relaxing, and resetting for whatever comes next.

Art has always been a part of me. I still remember standing on that stage in 8th grade, hearing my name called for first place at the art award ceremony; that moment stuck with me; it was more than just a ribbon or a certificate; it was proof that my creativity could make an impact. Even though gaming has taken up a lot of my free time these days, I find myself daydreaming about picking up a brush again; sketching or painting something that comes straight from my imagination. Creativity is still alive in me, even if life sometimes nudges it to the side.

I’m the kind of person who’s motivated by what’s ahead; by possibilities. I don’t have a single, specific long-term goal I’m chasing right now, but I’ve always believed in committing to what you start and not giving up halfway. That mindset has carried me through challenges; like the time I got stranded on a boat in the middle of a lake. I had to swim and push it back to shore; it was exhausting and terrifying, but it reminded me that staying calm and figuring things out can get you through almost anything.

Two teachers left a lasting mark on me; my 9th-grade art teacher and my 9th-grade biology teacher. They encouraged me to explore my passions and challenged me to see things differently. I still carry lessons from them in the way I approach both life and work.

But more than anything, I value laughter, friendships, and the small, shared moments that turn into memories. People often joke that I live for dad jokes; like the time I told everyone I dreamed I was floating in a “Fanta sea.” Those little moments of humor; those bursts of joy; are what make life fun and meaningful to me. I try to bring a little positivity and light into every day; whether it’s through a joke, a smile, or just being there for someone.

At the end of the day, that’s really me; a Tennessean, a creator, a problem-solver, a friend, and someone who tries to make life a little brighter; one laugh, one sketch, or one small memory at a time.

“You should never give up on what you are trying to accomplish.”
